Our modules

Modul 1

Discusses the fundamental concepts of optical fiber and the measurement of Numerical Aperture (NA). This module explains the structure and working principles of optical fiber, including how light propagates through the fiber core. It also introduces the concept of NA as an important parameter that determines the light acceptance capability of the fiber. Students perform practical measurements of NA to understand the basic characteristics of optical fiber directly. The purpose of this module is to build a strong theoretical foundation before moving to measurement and installation practices.

Modul 2

Focuses on optical fiber network performance measurement techniques using instruments such as OTDR and OPM. Students learn how OTDR is used to detect faults, measure fiber length, and determine attenuation along the transmission path. In addition, the Optical Power Meter (OPM) is used to measure optical power levels. Through hands-on practice and data analysis, students are trained to interpret measurement results for evaluating network quality and troubleshooting purposes.

Modul 3

Covers optical fiber splicing techniques using a fusion splicer. This module explains the working principles of the fusion splicer and the correct splicing procedures to achieve minimal loss at the joint. Students perform practical splicing activities and evaluate the splice quality. The module emphasizes technical skills required for proper installation and maintenance of optical fiber networks to ensure optimal system performance.
